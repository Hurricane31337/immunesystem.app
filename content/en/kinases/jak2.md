---
title: JAK2 — Hematopoiesis and Immune Signals
category: Kinase
color: "#22bb55"
---

JAK2 has a dual role: It is essential for blood cell formation (hematopoiesis) AND for certain immune signals. At the erythropoietin receptor (EPO-R → STAT5), it controls red blood cell production; at the thrombopoietin receptor (TPO-R → STAT5), platelet production. In the immune system, it participates in the IL-12 receptor (with TYK2 → STAT4, important for Th1 cells), IL-23 receptor (with TYK2 → STAT3/STAT4, important for Th17 cells), IFN-γ receptor (with JAK1 → STAT1), and IL-6 receptor (with JAK1 → STAT3).

The **JAK2-V617F mutation** (a single amino acid substitution) leads to constitutive (permanent) JAK2 activation → uncontrolled blood cell production → polycythemia vera (too many red blood cells), essential thrombocythemia (too many platelets), or primary myelofibrosis (scarring of the bone marrow).

**JAK2 inhibitors**: Ruxolitinib (inhibits JAK1 and JAK2) is approved for myelofibrosis, polycythemia vera, and steroid-refractory GvHD (graft-versus-host disease); topically also for vitiligo and atopic dermatitis. Baricitinib (JAK1/2) for RA, atopic dermatitis, and alopecia areata (patchy hair loss). Fedratinib (JAK2-selective) for myelofibrosis.

Important limitation: Since EPO and TPO signal through JAK2, JAK2 inhibitors can cause anemia and thrombocytopenia (low platelet count) — this is a dose-limiting side effect.
