---
title: JAK1 — The Most Versatile Janus Kinase
category: Kinase
color: "#4488ff"
---

JAK1 is the most broadly utilized Janus kinase — it participates in the signaling pathways of particularly many cytokines. It sits together with a partner JAK at the receptor: With JAK2 at the IFN-γ receptor and IL-6 receptor/gp130; with JAK3 at the IL-4 receptor, IL-2 receptor, IL-7 receptor, and IL-21 receptor (all γc chain receptors); and with TYK2 at the IFN-α/β receptor, IL-13 receptor, and IL-10 receptor.

This broad involvement means: A JAK1 inhibitor blocks many cytokine signaling pathways simultaneously — which is therapeutically powerful but can also cause more side effects. Recently, so-called JAK1 gain-of-function variants (overactive JAK1 mutations) were discovered in about 60 patients, leading to a syndrome with autoimmunity, atopy (allergic predisposition), colitis (intestinal inflammation), and dermatitis (skin inflammation).

The most important JAK1-selective inhibitors are: **Upadacitinib** (approved for RA, psoriatic arthritis, atopic dermatitis, spondyloarthritis, and inflammatory bowel diseases — one of the broadest approval spectrums), **Filgotinib** (RA, IBD), and **Abrocitinib** (atopic dermatitis). Their efficacy across so many different diseases is directly explained by JAK1's broad involvement in various cytokine signaling pathways.
