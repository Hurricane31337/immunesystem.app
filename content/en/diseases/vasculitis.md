Vasculitis is a collective term for diseases in which the immune system attacks blood vessels. Depending on the size of affected vessels: **Large vessel vasculitis** (giant cell arteritis, Takayasu arteritis) → affects the aorta and large arteries. **Medium vessels** (polyarteritis nodosa). **Small vessel vasculitis** → ANCA-associated vasculitides (granulomatosis with polyangiitis/GPA, microscopic polyangiitis/MPA, eosinophilic granulomatosis/EGPA) and immune complex-mediated vasculitides.

In ANCA-associated vasculitides, the body forms antibodies against neutrophil enzymes: anti-PR3 (proteinase 3) in GPA and anti-MPO (myeloperoxidase) in MPA. These ANCA antibodies activate neutrophils, which then adhere to vessel walls and release reactive oxygen species, proteases, and NETs → vessel wall damage → necrotizing vasculitis. In giant cell arteritis, Th1 cells (IFN-γ) and Th17 cells (IL-17) play central roles; macrophages fuse into giant cells in the vessel wall. IL-6 is a key cytokine (JAK1/JAK2 → STAT3).

**Biologics**: **Rituximab** (anti-CD20) is approved for ANCA-associated vasculitis (GPA, MPA) — for both induction and maintenance therapy. It depletes B cells that produce ANCA antibodies. **Avacopan** (C5a receptor antagonist) is approved for ANCA vasculitis and blocks complement activation that activates neutrophils at vessel walls. **Mepolizumab** (anti-IL-5) is approved for EGPA and reduces pathogenic eosinophils. **Tocilizumab** (anti-IL-6R) is approved for giant cell arteritis — blocks the JAK1/JAK2 → STAT3 signaling pathway and significantly reduces inflammation and relapse risk. Benralizumab (anti-IL-5Rα) is being studied in EGPA.
