The IL-23/IL-17 axis is a signaling pathway that has been identified over the past 15 years as a central driver of many autoimmune diseases — particularly psoriasis.

**IL-23** is a cytokine (signaling molecule) consisting of two subunits: p19 and p40 (it shares the p40 subunit with IL-12). It is mainly PRODUCED by conventional dendritic cells (cDC) and macrophages (arrow: cDC/macrophage → IL-23). IL-23 binds to a receptor complex of IL-23R and IL-12Rβ1 on Th17 cells. Inside the cell, it activates JAK2 and TYK2, which phosphorylate STAT3 (primarily) and STAT4. The crucial function of IL-23 is NOT the initiation of Th17 differentiation (that is done by IL-6 + TGF-β), but the stabilization and expansion of already differentiated Th17 cells — it keeps them alive and multiplies them.

**IL-17A** and **IL-17F** are PRODUCED by Th17 cells (arrow: Th17 → IL-17), but also by other cell types such as γδ T cells, ILC3 (Innate Lymphoid Cells), and even neutrophils and mast cells. IL-17 binds to the receptor complex IL-17RA/IL-17RC, which is found on many cell types, especially keratinocytes and fibroblasts. Important: The IL-17 signaling pathway does NOT use the JAK/STAT pathway, but the adapter ACT1 → TRAF6 → NF-κB and MAPK.

Effects of IL-17 on keratinocytes: Production of CXCL1 and CXCL8 (attract neutrophils), CCL20 (attracts more dendritic cells and Th17 cells → amplification loop), antimicrobial peptides (β-defensins, S100 proteins), keratinocyte proliferation (together with IL-22 via STAT3), and production of MMPs (tissue remodeling enzymes).

Clinical significance: In psoriasis, the IL-23/IL-17 axis is the proven central disease mechanism, and genetic polymorphisms in the IL-23 receptor gene are an established risk factor. In rheumatoid arthritis, IL-17 synergistically enhances TNF-α effects (via IκBζ) and promotes bone destruction. In spondyloarthritis and psoriatic arthritis, this axis is also prominent. Important limitation: In Crohn's disease, blocking IL-17 can paradoxically worsen the disease, as IL-17 also has a protective barrier function in the gut — therefore anti-IL-23 antibodies are preferred there.

**Drugs**: Anti-IL-23 (p19 subunit): Risankizumab, Guselkumab, Tildrakizumab. Anti-IL-12/23 (p40 subunit): Ustekinumab (blocks IL-12 AND IL-23). Anti-IL-17A: Secukinumab, Ixekizumab. Anti-IL-17A+F: Bimekizumab. Anti-IL-17 receptor: Brodalumab (blocks IL-17A, F, C, and E).
